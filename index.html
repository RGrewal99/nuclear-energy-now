Why Nuclear Energy?

Nuclear energy is a clean, safe, and reliable source of power that can help us combat climate change and reduce our dependence on fossil fuels.

Zero Emissions

Nuclear power plants produce no greenhouse gas emissions during operation, making them a key solution for climate change.

Energy Density

The energy density of nuclear fuel allows it to generate massive amounts of electricity with minimal land use, making it an efficient source of energy.

Reliability

Nuclear plants operate at full capacity 93% of the time, providing a reliable baseload of energy regardless of weather conditions.

Safety

Nuclear power is one of the safest forms of power generation, with multiple layers of redundancy and advanced safety systems designed to prevent accidents and protect the environment.

Lifespan

Nuclear power plants have an impressive lifespan of 40 to over 60 years, with the ability to extend their lives to 100 years, allowing them to provide consistent and reliable energy for decades while serving as cost-effective long-term investments.

Energy Security

Nuclear energy strengthens energy security by providing a stable, long-term power supply that reduces reliance on imported fuels, ensures grid stability, and supports national energy independence.

Calling out the liars

At Nuclear Energy Now, we're tired of the misinformation launched by so-called 'Green' activists. We want to call out these organisations that engage in spreading lies to make the public fearful of nuclear energy.


  • Greenpeace has historically opposed nuclear energy, arguing that it is unsafe, expensive, and produces hazardous waste. However, some of their claims have been challenged by experts who argue that nuclear power is a reliable and low-carbon energy source. For example, Greenpeace has cited high death toll estimates from the Chernobyl disaster, suggesting that up to 200,000 people may have died due to radiation exposure. However, the World Health Organization (WHO) estimated a much lower number, with around 4,000 potential radiation-related deaths among exposed populations. Greenpeace's figures significantly overstate the impact.

  • The Sierra Club has strongly opposed in particular California's Diablo Canyon Nuclear Power Plant, citing concerns over safety, cost, and environmental impact. They argue that its proximity to earthquake faults makes it inherently risky, despite extensive engineering measures ensuring resilience. Additionally, they claim nuclear energy is too expensive and advocate for a full transition to renewables like wind and solar, even though Diablo Canyon provides reliable, carbon-free electricity that stabilizes the grid when renewables fluctuate. Their push to shut down the plant has raised concerns that California would become more dependent on natural gas, increasing carbon emissions instead of reducing them.

  • Green Parties: The German Green Party has faced criticism for spreading misinformation about the safety and feasibility of nuclear power plants in Germany. Reports suggest that key technical assessments were altered or downplayed to justify the shutdown of the country's remaining nuclear facilities, even as energy prices soared during the Ukraine crisis. Critics argue that the party exaggerated safety risks, despite Germany's nuclear plants being recognized for their robust safety measures. Additionally, the decision to phase out nuclear energy has been linked to increased reliance on coal and natural gas, which are more carbon-intensive.
