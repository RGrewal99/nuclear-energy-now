Why Nuclear Energy?

Nuclear energy is a clean, safe, and reliable source of power that can help us combat climate change and reduce our dependence on fossil fuels.

Zero Emissions

Nuclear power plants produce no greenhouse gas emissions during operation, making them a key solution for climate change.

Energy Density

The energy density of nuclear fuel allows it to generate massive amounts of electricity with minimal land use, making it an efficient source of energy.

Reliability

Nuclear plants operate at full capacity 93% of the time, providing a reliable baseload of energy regardless of weather conditions.

Safety

Nuclear power is one of the safest forms of power generation, with multiple layers of redundancy and advanced safety systems designed to prevent accidents and protect the environment.

Lifespan

Nuclear power plants have an impressive lifespan of 40 to over 60 years, with the ability to extend their lives to 100 years, allowing them to provide consistent and reliable energy for decades while serving as cost-effective long-term investments.

Energy Security

Nuclear energy strengthens energy security by providing a stable, long-term power supply that reduces reliance on imported fuels, ensures grid stability, and supports national energy independence.
